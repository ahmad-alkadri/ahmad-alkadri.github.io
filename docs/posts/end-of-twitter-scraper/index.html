So long, and thanks for all the tweets

As of yesterday, and confirmed by Elon Musk himself (kinda), Twitter requires people to be signed in to see tweets, even public ones. End of an era.
Sat, Jul 1, 2023

Somebody poked me yesterday and told me that my Tweet Cloud Maker hasn’t been working well for whole morning, until finally stopped altogether–just returning warnings of empty results. That was kinda unsurprising to me at first. I just finished working on fixing and rewriting the backend tweet scraper a couple weeks back, and I figured it’d be just one of those bugs that I’d need to fix.
Screenshot of the app when I checked it

After almost an hour of debugging and no result, I finally turned to my most trusted aide: Google. Almost immediately, I got the answer to my question: Twitter now requires an account to view tweets.
Screenshot from the article

After reading the article, I immediately headed over to my next most trusted source of news: Twitter itself. Indeed, users have been apparently noticing this and complaining about it. Everybody now has to be registered and logged in to view tweets. Even from public twitter user. But how about twitter API, especially v1.1?

Well I launched my Postman almost immediately to check. Expected at least some successes, at least they’d warn everyone before doing something as impactful as revoking one of the most important functionalities of their own publicly available API, right? Something as breaking as this?

Wrong again. The API v1.1 does not work anymore to scrape tweets.
Screenshot of the API call to get the guest token
Screenshot of the API call to get the tweets

This is really worldwide changes. Effectively this ends all twitter scraper. Even those that have been used since a longtime by so many people like Nitter. And yes, the developers of Nitter have realized this too–the repo has been alight with discussions.

Today, Musk finally confirmed, via Twitter, the changes that have happpened. Well, kinda, though, because he didn’t directly addressed it; rather, he said something that I think even worse: all accounts on Twitter are now rate-limited, with the newly unverified accounts can only read 300 tweets per day, old unverified accounts 600 tweets per day, and finally, verified accounts up to 6000 tweets per day.
Mind you, to get yourself verified on Twitter, you’ll have to pay.

Musk is saying that these changes are done to address “extreme levels of data scraping & system manipulation”, but honestly, I don’t buy it. Data scraping is practically unavoidable in the open internet; from search engine to hobbyist (like myself) and even government agencies everybody is doing it, and there are ways to handle it, to prevent it from overburdening your system. IP limiters, device detections… seriously, you can even implement it easily on your app with Flask-Limiter. I don’t see how a company as big as Twitter cannot implement one of these alternative solutions, anything other than what they’re doing right now…

Anyway, Musk has went on to said that these changes are probably “temporary”. Well, I don’t know if it’s gonna be temporary or not, can’t really trust the words of “higher-ups” these days, but for now I’ve decided to put Tweet Cloud Maker to sleep and put my work on pytterrator on pause. With Nitter also not functioning, I can only say:

So long, and thanks for all the tweets
